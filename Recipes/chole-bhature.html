Chole bhature

Ingredient required

For chole Curry

  • 1 cup dried chickpeas (soaked overnight)
  • 1 large onion (finely chopped)
  • 2 tomatoes (finely chopped or pureed)
  • 2 green chilies (slit)
  • 1-inch ginger (grated)
  • 4-5 garlic cloves (finely chopped)
  • 2-3 tbsp oil or ghee
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 black cardamom
  • 2-3 cloves
  • 1-inch cinnamon stick
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1 tbsp chole masala (store-bought or homemade)
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 1 tsp amchur (dry mango powder) or lemon juice (for tanginess)
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves (for garnish)

For fluffy bhature

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 2 tbsp semolina (sooji or rava)
  • 2 tbsp yogurt (curd)
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp baking powder
  • Water (for kneading)
  • Oil (for deep frying)

Step by step recipe to make delicious Chole Bhature at home

Step 1 : Preparing the chole (chickpea curry)

  1. Soak the chickpeas :
    • Soak 1 cup of dried chickpeas in enough water overnight or for at least 6-8 hours.
  2. Cook the chickpeas:
    • Drain the soaked chickpeas and pressure cook them with 3-4 cups of water and a little salt for 4-5 whistles, or until they are soft but not mushy.
  3. Prepare the Masala Base :
    • In a large pan, heat 2-3 tbsp of oil or ghee. Add cumin seeds, bay leaf, black cardamom, cloves, and cinnamon. Sauté until the spices release their aroma.
    • Add finely chopped onions and cook them until they turn golden brown.
    • Add ginger, garlic, and green chilies. Sauté for another 2 minutes until the raw smell disappears.
    • Now, add the chopped tomatoes or tomato puree and cook until the oil starts separating from the masala.
  4. Add the Spices :
    • Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, chole masala, coriander powder, and salt. Cook the masala for 2-3 minutes on low heat.
  5. Mix the Chickpeas :
    • Once the masala is well-cooked, add the cooked chickpeas along with the water they were boiled in. Mix everything well and let it simmer for 10-15 minutes. Add more water if you prefer a thinner consistency.
  6. Finshing Touches:
    • Add garam masala, amchur powder (or lemon juice), and adjust salt if necessary. Let it simmer for another 2-3 minutes.
    • Garnish with fresh coriander leaves.

Step 2 : Preparing Bhature (Fried Bread)

  1. Knead the Dough :
    • In a large bowl, combine all-purpose flour, semolina, sugar, salt, and baking powder. Add yogurt and mix.
    • Gradually add water and knead the mixture into a smooth and soft dough. The dough should not be too hard or too sticky.
  2. Rest the Dough :
    • Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for 1-2 hours.
  3. Shape the Bhature:
    • After the dough has rested, divide it into small equal-sized balls.
    • Roll each ball into a circle of medium thickness using a rolling pin. Make sure the Bhature are not too thin, or they won’t puff up.
  4. Fry the Bhature:
    • Heat oil in a deep frying pan. Once the oil is hot, carefully slide one Bhatura into the oil. Press it gently with a slotted spoon to help it puff up.
    • Fry until golden brown on both sides. Remove and drain on paper towels.

Step 3: Serve

Now you tasty chole bhature is ready to serve. serve them with pickle and aloo ki sabzi to enhance the taste of it.
