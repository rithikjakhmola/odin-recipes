Pizza

Ingredients:

For the Dough:

  • 500g (4 cups) all-purpose flour or "00" pizza flour
  • 325ml (1⅓ cups) warm water
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 7g (1 packet) dry yeast
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1½ tsp salt

For the Sauce:

  • 400g (14 oz) canned San Marzano tomatoes (or any peeled tomatoes)
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 garlic clove, minced
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh basil leaves (optional)
  • Pinch of oregano (optional)

For the Toppings:

  • 250g (9 oz) fresh mozzarella cheese (preferably buffalo mozzarella)
  • Fresh basil leaves
  • Olive oil for drizzling
  • Optional: thinly sliced vegetables, prosciutto, mushrooms, or other toppings of your choice

Instruction

  1. Prepare the Dough:
    1. Activate the yeast: In a small bowl, combine the warm water, sugar, and dry yeast. Stir and let it sit for 5-10 minutes until it becomes frothy.
    2. Mix the dough: In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour and salt. Make a well in the center, pour in the yeast mixture and olive oil, and mix until a sticky dough forms.
    3. Knead: Transfer the dough to a lightly floured surface and knead for about 10 minutes until smooth and elastic. The dough should be soft and slightly sticky but not too wet.
    4. Let it rise: Place the dough in an oiled bowl, cover with a damp cloth, and let it rise in a warm place for 1-2 hours until it doubles in size.
  2. Prepare the Sauce :
    1. Heat olive oil in a saucepan over medium heat. Add minced garlic and sauté until fragrant, about 30 seconds.
    2. Add the canned tomatoes, crushing them with your hands or a spoon. Season with salt, pepper, and a pinch of oregano if desired.
    3. Simmer for about 10-15 minutes until slightly thickened. Set aside to cool.
  3. Preheat the Oven:
    1. Preheat your oven to the highest temperature possible (usually 250°C/475°F) for at least 30 minutes. If using a pizza stone, place it in the oven while it preheats.
  4. Shape the Dough
    1. Once the dough has risen, divide it into 2-4 equal portions, depending on how big you want your pizzas.
    2. Roll out each portion into a thin circle or stretch it by hand, leaving a slightly thicker edge for the crust.
  5. Assemble the Pizza:
    1. Spread a thin layer of the tomato sauce over the dough.
    2. Tear the mozzarella cheese into pieces and scatter them over the sauce.
    3. Add fresh basil leaves and drizzle with olive oil.
  6. Bake the Pizza:
    1. Slide the pizza onto a preheated pizza stone or a baking sheet.
    2. Bake for 7-10 minutes or until the crust is golden and the cheese is melted and bubbly.
    3. Remove from the oven, drizzle with more olive oil if desired, and add a few more fresh basil leaves.
  7. Serve
    1. Slice the pizza and enjoy immediately!

Tips:

  • For an authentic Italian pizza, less is more when it comes to toppings. Stick with a few high-quality ingredients.
  • Let the dough rest for longer (overnight in the fridge) for a more flavorful crust.
